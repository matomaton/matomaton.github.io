Motion Guidelines for the Pharos Design System

Summary

I took ownership of the process of bringing motion design to our company's design system.We kicked off with a series of collaborative workshops across our design, product, and engineering teams to align on core principles that would guide motion in the system. I facilitated these sessions and synthesized the discussions into draft motion guidelines.

I actively collaborated with stakeholders and incorporated cross-functional feedback to refine the guidelines. Once finalized, I created a comprehensive technical guide aligned with the principles, getting it validated by both designers and developers.

A key win was influencing the engineers to build an evaluation environment for testing the motion design tokens on UI components. This hands-on prototyping was invaluable for iterating motion studies aligned with the teams' vision.

Goal

Create a well reasoned direction for applying motion to our design system and brand design at large.

Exploration

To establish a foundation for applying motion to our brand's design system, I first benchmarked how other leading design systems have implemented motion. This included in-depth analysis of motion usage in systems like Ant Design, IBM Carbon, and Monday. The insights from studying these systems provided a framework to approach adding motion that aligns with our brand identity and use cases.

Brand Alignment

During my exploration of motion best practices, I discovered a key principle of separating motion into two categories: "Productive Motion" and "Expressive Motion". To apply this to our brand, I led a workshop where we organized our existing 32 brand concepts into these two motion types. First, we reviewed each concept and determined whether it aligned with productive or expressive motion. Next, we removed any concepts that did not have a clear connection to motion. Finally, for the remaining concepts, we wrote descriptions of how each specifically relates to and informs motion design. I presented this conceptual motion framework to the broader team for feedback and refinement. Categorizing our brand concepts into productive and expressive motion types provided a strong foundation for bringing purposeful motion to life aligned with our brand identity.

First Draft

To establish consistent motion design patterns, I first drafted motion guidelines covering productive and expressive motion principles. I then refined these guidelines through stakeholder reviews and feedback to finalize best practices for each motion type. Finally, I created specific duration and easing examples demonstrating how to properly apply the guidelines to different components. This equips teams to add uniform motion to new components that aligns with our motion language.

Easing and Duration

Our design system started off with duration tokens, but no guidance on how and when to apply them. Additionally, there were no easing values. Elements on the screen should move naturally, speeding up and slowing down, following the physics of lightweight materials. Every movement should be smooth to enhance the perception of responsiveness, efficiency, and speed on the platform.

Evaluation Environment

Our design system started off with duration tokens but no guidance on how and when to apply them. Additionally there were no easing values. We wanted to include both entrance and exit easing.

Enter easing refers to elements gently fading or sliding into view, accelerating from zero to full movement/opacity. This makes the appearance naturalExit easing means elements will gradually fade or slide away, decelerating from full to zero movement/opacity. This makes the disappearance smooth.

Easing follows an "ease curve" that starts slow, accelerates, then slows again. This acceleration and deceleration creates lifelike, polished transitions between interface states for a seamless, responsive user experience.
